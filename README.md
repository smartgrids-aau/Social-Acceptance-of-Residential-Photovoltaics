# Social Acceptance of Residential Photovoltaics
PV  •  Energy Planning  •  Social Acceptance  •  Energy Modeling

This project explores the role of social acceptance in residential photovoltaic (PV) adoption and its impact on future energy planning. A literature review will analyze key factors such as willingness to pay, policy acceptance, environmental attitudes, and perceived barriers. These insights will be integrated into an energy system model and a simulation that serves as a tool to assess the share of residential PV in meeting future energy demand and the need for additional PV capacity.  The findings will inform policies that align public interest with technical feasibility for sustainable energy expansion.

## Research Questions
*	What social, economic, and attitudinal factors most strongly predict residential PV adoption?
*	How does modeled social acceptance affect projected PV penetration rates in energy system scenarios?
*	What policy instruments most effectively close the gap between public interest and actual deployment?
